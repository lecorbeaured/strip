# Best Things to Do on the Las Vegas Strip

The Las Vegas Strip is one of the most electrifying stretches of road on Earth. Stretching roughly 4.2 miles along Las Vegas Boulevard South, it's home to some of the world's most iconic hotels, world-class entertainment, Michelin-starred restaurants, and experiences you simply can't find anywhere else. Whether it's your first visit or your tenth, there's always something new to discover.

Here's our insider guide to making the most of every moment on the Strip.

## 1. Watch the Bellagio Fountains (Free)

No trip to the Strip is complete without watching the Bellagio Fountain Show. Every 15–30 minutes (depending on time of day), more than 1,200 water jets shoot streams up to 460 feet in the air, choreographed to iconic songs ranging from Sinatra to Celine Dion. The show is **completely free** and best viewed from the sidewalk along Las Vegas Boulevard.

Pro tip: The fountains are magical at sunset, and positively jaw-dropping after dark. Arrive 10 minutes early for a front-row spot.

## 2. Experience the Sphere

One of the newest and most mind-blowing additions to the Strip, **the Sphere** at The Venetian Resort is unlike anything you've experienced. The world's largest spherical structure features an 18,000-seat venue wrapped in a 160,000 square foot LED exterior and a fully immersive interior display. Shows and concerts sell out fast — book ahead.

## 3. Ride the High Roller Observation Wheel

Standing 550 feet tall, the **High Roller** at the LINQ Promenade is the tallest observation wheel in the world. Each air-conditioned cabin holds up to 40 people and makes one full rotation in about 30 minutes — giving you breathtaking 360° views of the Las Vegas Valley. Go at sunset for the most spectacular panorama, or at night for a sea of city lights.

[Book High Roller tickets here](https://www.viator.com/searchResults/all?text=high+roller+las+vegas)

## 4. See a World-Class Show

Las Vegas is the live entertainment capital of the world. The Strip is home to:

- **Residencies** from A-list artists (Adele, Katy Perry, Bruno Mars have all performed here)
- **Cirque du Soleil** shows like O at the Bellagio — a breathtaking aquatic spectacle
- **Magic and comedy** from legends like Penn & Teller and David Copperfield
- **The Blue Man Group** at Luxor for a sensory-overload performance like no other

Book early, especially for weekend shows — the best seats disappear fast.

## 5. Explore the Grand Canal Shoppes at the Venetian

The Venetian's **Grand Canal Shoppes** recreate Venice indoors, complete with gondoliers singing Italian opera as they pole you through indoor canals under a painted sky. It's surprisingly romantic and genuinely beautiful — and it's free to stroll through even if you don't ride the gondola.

## 6. Visit the Eiffel Tower Experience at Paris Las Vegas

For stunning views of the Strip from above, take the elevator to the top of the **Eiffel Tower replica** at Paris Las Vegas. At 46 stories tall, it offers panoramic views of the surrounding valley — and you can see the Bellagio fountains directly across the street. It's especially beautiful at night.

## 7. Take a Grand Canyon Day Trip

Just 4–5 hours from the Strip, the **Grand Canyon** is one of the most awe-inspiring natural wonders in the world. A guided tour from Las Vegas lets you skip the logistics and enjoy the journey — many tours stop at Hoover Dam en route. Helicopter tours to the canyon floor are the ultimate splurge.

## 8. Hop on the Las Vegas Monorail or Deuce Bus

The **Las Vegas Monorail** runs along the east side of the Strip and is a great way to cover ground quickly — especially in summer when the heat makes walking several blocks brutal. The **Deuce bus** on Las Vegas Boulevard runs 24 hours and covers the entire Strip for a flat fare.

## 9. Try Celebrity Chef Restaurants

Some of the world's best chefs have flagship restaurants on the Strip:

- **Gordon Ramsay Hell's Kitchen** at Caesars Palace
- **Joël Robuchon** at MGM Grand (for a true splurge)
- **Guy Fieri's Vegas Kitchen & Bar** at The LINQ for comfort food
- **Bobby's Burgers** for a casual bite between attractions

Book restaurant reservations well in advance, especially for weekend dinners.

## 10. Try Your Luck at the Casinos

The Strip's casinos are attractions in themselves — with spectacular interiors, free-flowing cocktails (for active players), and an atmosphere you won't find anywhere else. Even if you're not a gambler, it's worth walking through the floors of:

- **The Bellagio** — legendary elegance
- **The Venetian/Palazzo** — palatial scale
- **Caesars Palace** — old-school Vegas glamour
- **The ARIA** — sleek, modern luxury

Most casinos also have free attractions inside: art installations, giant aquariums (at Mandalay Bay), and the famous **Conservatory & Botanical Gardens** at the Bellagio, which changes with the seasons.

## Planning Tips

- **Best time to visit:** Spring (March–May) and Fall (September–November) offer the best weather. Summer is brutally hot but packed with deals.
- **Getting around:** The Strip looks walkable on a map but is deceiving — budget 30+ minutes to walk from one end to the other. Use Lyft/Uber between properties.
- **Crowds:** Friday and Saturday nights are the busiest. If you prefer a quieter experience, arrive Sunday through Thursday.
- **Budget:** You can experience much of the Strip for free, or spend thousands per night — it's entirely up to you.
