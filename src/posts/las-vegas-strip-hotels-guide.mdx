# Las Vegas Strip Hotels: The Complete Guide

Choosing the right hotel is one of the most important decisions you'll make for your Vegas trip. The Strip has hundreds of thousands of hotel rooms ranging from budget-friendly motels to some of the most opulent resorts on the planet. Here's everything you need to know to pick the perfect base for your visit.

## The Strip's Hotel Tiers

### Ultra-Luxury (From $400+/night)

**Wynn Las Vegas & Encore**
Widely regarded as the most refined properties on the Strip, Wynn and Encore set the standard for luxury. Expect lush gardens, world-class pools, private nightclub access, exceptional dining, and guest rooms with floor-to-ceiling windows looking out over the Strip or the manicured resort grounds. The service is genuinely exceptional.

**Bellagio**
The Bellagio is synonymous with Vegas elegance. Home to the iconic fountain show, a stunning conservatory, a world-class spa, and an art gallery, it's a resort experience that stands alone. The casino is beautiful, and the restaurant lineup — including Picasso and Prime Steakhouse — is unmatched.

**ARIA Resort & Casino**
ARIA is the most modern and technologically advanced resort on the Strip. The rooms are stunning, the pool complex is superb, and the dining lineup includes Carbone, one of the hottest Italian restaurants in America. It's sleek, smart, and supremely comfortable.

### Mid-Range (From $150–$400/night)

**The Venetian & Palazzo**
The Venetian offers spacious suites (the smallest rooms are 650 sq ft) with sunken living rooms — incredible value for the quality. The Grand Canal Shoppes, a massive casino, and excellent restaurants make this a full-resort experience. Often available at surprisingly competitive rates during the week.

**Caesars Palace**
Few hotels have the name recognition of Caesars. The Forum Shops, multiple pools, a world-class spa, and constant entertainment make this a classic Strip destination. Room quality varies significantly by tower — ask for the Nobu or Julius tower for the best rooms.

**Paris Las Vegas**
Paris offers a romantic, European-inspired atmosphere with the Eiffel Tower Experience right on property. Rooms are comfortable if not quite as large as the Venetian, and the location at the center of the Strip is ideal. Good value mid-week.

**New York-New York**
One of the most fun hotels on the Strip, with the Big Apple Roller Coaster on the roof, an amazing dueling piano bar, and great dining. Rooms have had recent renovations and the price point is reasonable.

### Budget-Friendly (Under $150/night)

**Excalibur Hotel & Casino**
Located at the south end of the Strip, Excalibur offers very affordable rates in a fun medieval-themed property. Rooms are basic but clean, and you're a short walk or tram ride from Luxor and Mandalay Bay. Great for first-timers on a budget.

**Circus Circus**
The most affordable property directly on the Strip, Circus Circus features a massive indoor theme park, a 100-foot-high trapeze act, and constant carnival games. Rooms are basic, but the free entertainment value is unmatched at this price point.

**Stratosphere (The STRAT)**
At the north end of the Strip, the Strat offers very competitive rates with the added bonus of thrill rides on top of the 1,149-foot tower. Not in the heart of the action, but the value is hard to beat.

## Key Considerations When Booking

### Location on the Strip Matters
The Strip runs roughly from Mandalay Bay (south) to the SLS/Sahara (north). Properties in the center — roughly from Bellagio to Cosmopolitan — command premium prices and put you within walking distance of the most popular attractions. South Strip and North Strip properties tend to be cheaper but require more transportation.

### Resort Fees
Every major Strip hotel charges a mandatory resort fee on top of the room rate — typically $35–$55 per night. These cover Wi-Fi, pool access, and other amenities but can significantly increase your total cost. Factor this in when comparing prices.

### Day-of-Week Pricing
Las Vegas hotel pricing is extremely dynamic. Weekends (Friday–Sunday) can cost 2–3x what the same room costs Monday–Thursday. If you have flexibility, a mid-week stay can save hundreds of dollars and mean smaller crowds.

## Which Hotel is Right for You?

- **Couples/honeymoon:** Wynn, Bellagio, or ARIA for romance and luxury
- **Party groups:** Cosmopolitan (adults-only pool), MGM Grand (Hakkasan), or Hard Rock
- **Families:** Excalibur, Circus Circus, or Luxor (all family-friendly with activities)
- **First-timers on a budget:** Venetian or Paris (good value, central location)
- **Business travelers:** ARIA or Palazzo (excellent meeting facilities and business amenities)
