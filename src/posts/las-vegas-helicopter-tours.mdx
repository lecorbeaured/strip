# Las Vegas Helicopter Tours: Everything You Need to Know

Few experiences in travel compare to seeing Las Vegas from the air. The neon grid of the Strip, the surrounding desert mountains, and — if you venture further — the impossible scale of the Grand Canyon. Las Vegas is one of the best cities in the world for helicopter tours, with departures available day and night from multiple locations.

Here's everything you need to know before you book.

## Types of Las Vegas Helicopter Tours

### 1. Las Vegas Strip Night Flight

The most popular option for first-timers is a **nighttime flight over the Las Vegas Strip**. Departing at dusk or after dark, these flights take you over the full length of Las Vegas Boulevard — from Mandalay Bay to the Stratosphere and beyond. Seeing the sea of lights from 1,000 feet up is genuinely breathtaking.

**Typical duration:** 12–15 minutes in the air  
**Typical price:** $90–$150 per person  
**Pickup:** Most operators offer hotel pickup from the Strip

### 2. Grand Canyon Helicopter Tour

The ultimate Las Vegas helicopter experience is a **flight to the Grand Canyon**. You depart from Las Vegas, fly over the Mojave Desert, cross Lake Mead, and arrive at the canyon rim — or even descend to the canyon floor for a champagne landing on the Colorado River.

Two main routes:
- **South Rim:** The most dramatic, deepest canyon views. About 4 hours total including ground time.
- **West Rim (Hualapai Nation):** Closer to Las Vegas (about 50 min flight), option to land on the canyon floor. More accessible for a day trip.

**Typical duration:** 3.5–5 hours total (45–90 min flight each way)  
**Typical price:** $350–$700+ per person depending on package

### 3. Combo Tours

Many operators offer combo packages that include:
- Helicopter flight over the Strip **plus** a Grand Canyon flight
- Helicopter **plus** a Hoover Dam boat tour
- Helicopter **plus** a Skywalk visit at the West Rim

These combo tours offer the best value per experience, especially if you're planning to do multiple activities anyway.

## Which Operator Should I Choose?

Several reputable operators run helicopter tours from Las Vegas:

**Papillon Grand Canyon Helicopters** — the largest helicopter tour company in the Southwest, with excellent safety record and a wide range of Grand Canyon packages.

**Maverick Helicopters** — highly rated for both Strip night flights and Grand Canyon tours, known for attentive service and modern aircraft.

**Vegas Dream Helicopters** — good budget option for Strip-only night flights.

When booking, look for:
- FAA Part 135 certified operators (required for commercial helicopter tours)
- Clear reviews on Viator or TripAdvisor
- What's included: hotel pickup, drinks, landing fee for Grand Canyon, etc.

## Best Time to Book

### For Strip Night Flights:
- Sunset and just-after-dark tours offer the most dramatic visual experience
- Friday and Saturday tours sell out weeks in advance
- Book at least 1–2 weeks ahead during peak season (March–May, September–November)

### For Grand Canyon Flights:
- Morning departures (7–10 AM) offer the best light for photography
- Summer (June–August) means longer days but extreme heat at the canyon floor
- Winter tours have the advantage of smaller crowds but cooler temperatures

## What to Expect on the Day

1. **Hotel pickup:** Most operators pick you up at your Strip hotel in a van — confirm pickup time and location when booking
2. **Check-in:** Arrive at the helicopter terminal 30–45 minutes early for check-in and safety briefing
3. **Weight requirements:** All passengers are weighed for load balancing — this is standard and necessary for flight safety
4. **Cameras:** Bring your camera or phone for photos; GoPro mounts are available on some aircraft
5. **Motion sickness:** If you're prone to it, take Dramamine 1 hour before the flight
6. **Dress code:** Comfortable clothes; closed-toe shoes required for Grand Canyon landings

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is it safe?**  
Commercial helicopter operators in the US are held to extremely strict FAA standards. The companies operating in Las Vegas have excellent safety records. That said, turbulence is common in the desert, and flights may be cancelled in high winds.

**What if the weather is bad?**  
Most operators offer full refunds or rescheduling for weather cancellations. Grand Canyon tours are more weather-dependent than Strip flights.

**Is there a weight limit?**  
Most operators have a 300 lb per person limit, with overweight seats available for purchase to accommodate larger passengers.

**Are children allowed?**  
Yes — most tours allow children with a paying adult. Infants under 2 may fly free on a parent's lap (check with specific operators).
