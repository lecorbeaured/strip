# Free Things to Do on the Las Vegas Strip

Las Vegas has a reputation as an expensive city — and it certainly can be. But some of the most memorable moments on the Strip cost absolutely nothing. From spectacular water shows to free circus acts, these free attractions are genuinely world-class entertainment.

## 1. Bellagio Fountain Show

The most iconic free attraction in Las Vegas, the **Bellagio Fountains** put on a choreographed water show every 15–30 minutes throughout the day and evening. Over 1,200 water jets, some shooting 460 feet in the air, dance in perfect synchronization to everything from Andrea Bocelli to Frank Sinatra.

**Schedule:**
- Weekdays: Every 30 minutes, 3:00 PM – 8:00 PM; every 15 minutes, 8:00 PM – midnight
- Weekends & holidays: Every 30 minutes, noon – 8:00 PM; every 15 minutes, 8:00 PM – midnight

The best viewing spot is the sidewalk along Las Vegas Boulevard directly in front of the Bellagio — it's free, it's spectacular, and it's a crowd favorite for good reason.

## 2. Mirage Volcano Show

The **Mirage Volcano** erupts every night at 8:00 PM and 9:00 PM (and 10:00 PM on Fridays and Saturdays), shooting real fireballs 100 feet into the air against a soundtrack by Grateful Dead drummer Mickey Hart and Indian tabla master Zakir Hussain. It's dramatic, it smells of fresh coconut (seriously), and it's completely free to watch from the sidewalk.

## 3. Fremont Street Experience (Free Light Show)

Technically just north of the Strip in Downtown Las Vegas, but absolutely worth the short trip: the **Fremont Street Experience** features a 1,500-foot LED canopy overhead that blasts hour-long light shows every night after dusk. The Viva Vision shows are free, spectacular, and unlike anything else in the world.

## 4. Bellagio Conservatory & Botanical Gardens

Inside the Bellagio, the **Conservatory & Botanical Gardens** is a massive, free display space where a team of 140 horticulturalists creates elaborate seasonal displays. Each theme (Chinese New Year, Spring, Summer, Fall, Holiday) features live flowers, massive sculptures, and intricate theming. It changes 5 times per year and is genuinely breathtaking.

Open 24 hours, free to enter — just walk through the Bellagio lobby.

## 5. The LINQ Promenade (Free to Explore)

The **LINQ Promenade** is an open-air shopping and entertainment district with free street performers, live music, and a fun atmosphere. While the High Roller observation wheel costs money, strolling the promenade, browsing the shops, and catching live acts is completely free.

## 6. MGM Grand Lion Habitat (CSI Experience Lobby)

While the live lions are no longer at the MGM Grand, the **MGM Grand's** spectacular grand entrance and spectacular casino interior are free to explore. Walk through the casino floor, check out the massive atrium, and soak in old-school Vegas glamour.

## 7. Caesars Palace Forum Shops

One of the world's most elaborate shopping malls, the **Forum Shops at Caesars Palace** features a replica of ancient Rome, complete with ornate fountains, a painted sky ceiling that shifts from day to night, and the famous **Atlantis Show** — an animatronic spectacle with fire effects that runs free throughout the day.

## 8. Flamingo Wildlife Habitat

The **Flamingo Las Vegas** hosts a free wildlife habitat on its property, with Chilean flamingos, pelicans, turtles, koi, and other wildlife living in a lush, tropical garden setting. It's open daily from 8 AM to dusk and is a surprisingly peaceful escape from the casino floor.

## 9. Wynn Atrium & Botanical Garden

The **Wynn Las Vegas** lobby features stunning botanical art installations that change seasonally — and the property itself, with its massive chandelier bar and art collection, is worth a wander. You don't need to be a guest to walk through the public areas.

## 10. Watch the World Go By on the Strip

Sometimes the best entertainment in Las Vegas is simply **people-watching from the Strip sidewalk**. The parade of costumes, characters, street performers, vendors, tourists, and locals is endlessly entertaining. Grab a drink to-go (it's legal to drink on the Strip) and find a good spot to soak it all in.

## Pro Tips for Free Vegas Fun

- **Walk the entire Strip at night** — the LED signs, neon lights, and illuminated hotel facades make it a visual spectacle on its own
- **Hotel lobbies are free to enter** — the Venetian, Bellagio, Wynn, and Cosmopolitan all have spectacular interiors
- **Free entertainment is everywhere** — live piano bars, lobby performances, and buskers at every corner
- **Bring a water bottle** — the Strip is long and hot in summer; free water refills are available at many hotel convenience shops
